What's an LLM? (1 Minute)

LLM = Large Language Model

The "engine" that powers AI tools

Think of it like cars:
‚Ä¢ LLM (ChatGPT, Claude, Grok) = engine
‚Ä¢ Model (GPT-5, Sonnet 4.5, etc.) = car

The Simple Truth: AI is sophisticated autocomplete. It predicts the next word based on patterns it learned from billions of text examples.

How it "sees" text: AI reads in chunks called tokens (not individual letters). "Hello world" = 2 tokens. This is why AI struggles with spelling/counting but excels at understanding meaning.

üí° Why this matters: Different engines (models) are good at different things‚Äîjust like car engines!

üí° How to Think About AI: The Zip File

AI has fuzzy memory from training, but can search the web for current info when you ask

üì¶ Long-Term Memory (Weights)
What it is: Compressed knowledge from training

Like: Vague recollection of a book you read last year

‚ö†Ô∏è Can be fuzzy, outdated, or wrong
üí¨ Working Memory (Context Window)
What it is: Whatever you just told it in this chat

Like: Having the book open right in front of you

‚úÖ Clear, immediate, reliable

üí° Pro Tip: Upload docs directly to chat. For current info, explicitly click "Search web" or AI may give outdated data.

The Prompting Recipe

1. ROLE: "You are a..."
2. CONTEXT: What you're working on
3. TASK: The job to do
4. CONSTRAINTS: Tone, length, audience
5. FORMAT: Bullets, table, etc.
6. EXAMPLES: Show samples (optional)
Real Example ‚Üì
1. ROLE ‚Üí You are an executive assistant
2. CONTEXT ‚Üí helping prepare for weekly team meetings.
3. TASK ‚Üí Summarize these meeting notes and identify action items.
4. CONSTRAINTS ‚Üí Keep summary under 150 words. Use professional tone.
5. FORMAT ‚Üí Output as: Summary (3 bullets), then Action Items table with columns: Owner | Task | Deadline
6. EXAMPLE ‚Üí
Good output looks like:
‚Ä¢ Key decision made
‚Ä¢ Main discussion point
‚Ä¢ Next steps agreed

Action Items:
| Sarah | Draft proposal | Friday |
üí° Best Practice: Be clear and specific (OpenAI/Claude). The more details you give, the better the output!

üí° Tip: At minimum, always include Task + Format. That's 80% of the value.

üé≠ Hallucinations: How to Catch Them

Why AI Hallucinates
AI is trained to always give an answer‚Äîeven when it doesn't know. It would rather make something up than say "I don't know."
üîç How to Catch Hallucinations
  • Ask for sources/citations
  • Cross-check with Google
  • Use search tools for facts
  • Ask AI: "How confident are you? What could be wrong?"
  • Compare outputs from 2 AIs
‚úÖ Prevention Strategies
  • Add to prompts: "Only use information I've provided"
  • Add: "If unsure, say 'I don't have enough information'"
  • Upload source documents
  • Use newer models (less hallucination)
  • Verify critical info always

üí° Rule of thumb: If it's important, verify it. If it's critical, verify it twice. If it's life/death or legal, don't rely on AI alone.
