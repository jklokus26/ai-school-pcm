‚õìÔ∏è Demo 1: Chained Prompts (Multi-Step Workflow)

üìã Task: Turn article ‚Üí Summary ‚Üí Expansion ‚Üí Tweets ‚Üí Newsletter
‚è±Ô∏è Time: ~5 minutes total
üõ†Ô∏è Tool: ChatGPT or Claude (either works)

Prompt 1: Summarize

Summarize this article in 5 key points. Each point should be one sentence, max 20 words. [Paste your article here]

Prompt 2: Expand One Point

Take point #2 from your summary and expand it into a 200-word explanation for a general audience. Make it engaging and easy to understand.

Prompt 3: Create Social Content

Turn that 200-word explanation into 3 tweet variations: - Tweet 1: Question format - Tweet 2: Stat/fact forward - Tweet 3: Actionable insight Each under 280 characters.

Prompt 4: Newsletter Intro

Now create an email newsletter introduction (exactly 100 words) using the 5-point summary from your first response. Include: - Engaging hook (1 sentence) - The 5 points briefly mentioned - Call-to-action to read full article Tone: professional but conversational.
‚úÖ What you've created: From one article, you now have: Summary, Blog post, 3 Tweets, and Newsletter intro‚Äîall in ~5 minutes!

üñºÔ∏è Demo 3: Image Verification (Two-Step Method)

Step 1: Transcribe FIRST

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: After AI responds, manually verify 3-5 values match the image exactly. Do NOT skip this step!

üí° Use cases: Nutrition labels, invoices, receipts, forms, charts, screenshots, medical results (non-diagnostic), product specs

üèõÔ∏è Demo 4: LLM Council (Multi-Model Comparison)

Question for All Models

I'm planning a 3-day team retreat for 15 people near [your city]. Budget: $2,000/person. Suggest 2 venue options with: - Location name and type - Key amenities for team building - Estimated cost breakdown - One unique advantage Keep each option under 100 words.
Testing sequence:
  1. Paste in ChatGPT ‚Üí get Answer A
  2. Paste in Claude ‚Üí get Answer B
  3. Compare side-by-side on screen
  4. Optional: Follow up: "Compare these two responses. What are the pros/cons of each recommendation?"
üí° When to use: Important decisions, creative brainstorming, when you're not sure which tool is better for a task

üß† Demo 5: Thinking Model Comparison

Hard Logic Problem

I have 8 identical-looking coins. One is counterfeit and weighs slightly less than the others. Using only a balance scale, what's the MINIMUM number of weighings needed to identify the counterfeit coin? Explain your strategy step-by-step.
Comparison test:
  1. Try with standard GPT-5 ‚Üí May get suboptimal answer (3 weighings)
  2. Try with o4-mini ‚Üí Should get optimal (2 weighings)
  3. Show thinking trace if available
üí° Correct answer: 2 weighings minimum. Thinking models are much more likely to get this right.

‚úÖ Verification Prompts (Add to Any Prompt)

General Fact-Checking

Important: Double-check all facts before responding. Flag any claims you're uncertain about with [VERIFY]. Provide sources for key facts where possible.

Source Citation Requirement

Cite sources for each major claim. Format: "Claim" (Source: URL or document name, page number). If no source available, note it's based on general knowledge and flag uncertainty level.

Assumption Declaration

List all assumptions you're making in your analysis. Format: Assumptions: 1. [First assumption and why you're making it] 2. [Second assumption and why you're making it] If any assumptions are uncertain, flag them for verification.

Document-Only Constraint

IMPORTANT: Only use information from the documents I've provided. Do not add information from your general knowledge unless explicitly asked. If the documents don't contain enough information to answer fully, say so.

Safe Data Analysis Prompt

You are a data analyst helping me understand this spreadsheet. Analyze the data and provide: 1. Top 3 trends (cite specific numbers) 2. Notable outliers or anomalies 3. Potential data quality issues (missing values, inconsistencies) IMPORTANT: - Show calculations for any percentages or growth rates - Flag any assumptions you make - If data seems incomplete, note what's missing - Double-check all numbers Format as: Findings (bullets) + Calculations (show work) + Caveats (what to verify)

Safe Document Analysis Prompt

You are an analyst reviewing this report. Provide: 1. Executive summary (3 bullets, 50 words max) 2. Key metrics and trends (cite page numbers) 3. Stated assumptions or limitations in the document itself 4. Gaps or questions the document doesn't address CRITICAL: - Only reference information explicitly stated in the document - Quote directly when making claims (include page numbers) - Distinguish between facts in the document vs your interpretations - Flag anything you're inferring vs directly stated Tone: Objective and analytical.

üîÑ Iteration & Refinement Prompts

Make More Specific

This is too generic. Make it more specific by: - Adding concrete examples - Citing specific data points or numbers - Removing vague language ("some," "many," "often") - Using precise terminology

Change Perspective

Rewrite this from the perspective of: - [Different audience: executives/technical team/customers/etc.] - Adjust language, detail level, and focus accordingly - Keep same core information, change presentation

Add Structure

Restructure this content with clear sections: 1. Context/Background (2 sentences) 2. Main Content (organized by subtopic) 3. Key Takeaways (3 bullets) 4. Next Steps or Recommendations Use headers for each section.

Get Multiple Versions

Give me 3 different versions of this content: Version A: Short and punchy (under 100 words) Version B: Detailed and comprehensive (300-400 words) Version C: Data-heavy with specific numbers and citations I'll pick the best one to refine further.

üéØ Real-World Workflow Examples

Workflow: Customer Feedback Analysis

Input: 50+ customer feedback responses
Output: Executive report with recommendations

Step 1: "Categorize this feedback into: Product Issues, Feature Requests, Praise, Complaints. Show count for each category."

Step 2: "For each category, list the top 3 most frequently mentioned items with example quotes."

Step 3: "Create a priority matrix: Impact (High/Med/Low) vs Effort (High/Med/Low) for the top issues and requests."

Step 4: "Draft an executive summary (300 words) with: Current sentiment, Top 3 priorities, Recommended actions."

Workflow: Competitive Intelligence

Input: Competitor websites and news
Output: Competitive analysis brief

Step 1: "Using web search, research companies A, B, and C. For each: recent news, product launches, market position." (Use Search tool)

Step 2: "Create comparison table: Company | Main Product | Target Market | Pricing | Unique Strength | Weakness"

Step 3: "Which competitor is the biggest threat to our position in [market segment]? Support with specific evidence."

Step 4: "Based on this analysis, recommend 3 strategic moves to compete more effectively."

Workflow: Document Synthesis

Input: 5 different reports on related topics
Output: Synthesized insights document

Step 1: Upload all documents to Claude Project ‚Üí "What themes appear across ALL documents?"

Step 2: "For each theme, provide: Definition, Evidence from each document (cite which doc), Consensus or disagreement."

Step 3: "Create a synthesis table: Theme | Evidence | Agreement Level | Implications"

Step 4: "Write a 400-word synthesis report: Common findings, Points of disagreement, Recommended next steps for further research."

üîß Troubleshooting Prompts

When AI Misunderstands

When Output Quality Is Poor

This output is too generic and not useful. Improve it by: 1. Being more specific (add examples, data, concrete details) 2. Removing filler language and vague statements 3. Making it actionable (what should I DO with this?) 4. Adding structure (organize logically) Rewrite with these improvements.
