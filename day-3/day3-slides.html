‚è∞ Reality Check: The Decade of Agents

The hype: "AI will replace all jobs this year!"
The reality: It's going to take about a decade.

‚úÖ What AI Can Do TODAY
  • Draft emails and documents
  • Summarize and extract data
  • Answer questions (with verification)
  • Help you code (with supervision)
  • Generate images and basic content
‚ö†Ô∏è What's Still MISSING
  • Continual learning (remembering between sessions)
  • True multimodal understanding
  • Full computer use (clicking, navigating)
  • Human-level judgment in complex scenarios
  • Reliable execution without errors

üí° What this means: AI is a powerful assistant TODAY. It's not replacing your job‚Äîit's making you faster. Use it wisely, verify outputs, and keep learning.

‚õìÔ∏è Chaining Prompts: Build Complex Outputs

Pro technique: Break big tasks into small steps

Example: Podcast ‚Üí Multi-Format Content
Prompt 1: "Summarize this podcast transcript in 5 key points"
‚Üí AI gives you structured summary

Prompt 2: "Take point #3 and expand it into a 300-word blog post"
‚Üí AI creates detailed content from one point

Prompt 3: "Now turn that blog post into 5 tweet variations"
‚Üí AI adapts for social media

Prompt 4: "Create an email newsletter intro using the summary from Prompt 1"
‚Üí AI references earlier output

üí° Key Insight: Each prompt builds on the last. AI keeps context from earlier in the chat. This is WAY more powerful than trying to do everything in one giant prompt!

üîç When You MUST Use Search Tools

‚ö†Ô∏è Without Search, AI Will Make Stuff Up!
AI's knowledge is 6+ months old. For anything recent or frequently changing, you MUST use search tools or AI will confidently hallucinate.
‚úÖ Use Search For:
  • News and current events
  • Product launches and updates
  • Stock prices and market data
  • Company information (new hires, acquisitions)
  • Schedules and release dates
  • Recent research papers
‚úÖ Skip Search For:
  • General knowledge (how things work)
  • Historical facts
  • Concept explanations
  • Writing tasks (emails, summaries)
  • Brainstorming ideas
  • Document analysis (you upload)

üí° Pro Tip: In ChatGPT, click "Search" button. In Perplexity, it searches automatically. When in doubt, search!

üìä Spreadsheet & Data Workflows

Workflow: Extract Insights from Data
Step 1: Upload CSV/Excel to Claude (handles best)
Step 2: "Summarize this data. What are the top 3 trends?"
Step 3: "Create a pivot table showing X by Y"
Step 4: "Which rows have values above [threshold]? List them."
Step 5: "Turn these findings into 3 executive bullets"
‚ö†Ô∏è ALWAYS Verify Numbers!
Example error: AI says "Revenue was $1.2M" but spreadsheet shows $1.02M

Solution: Spot-check a few values. If AI gets basic numbers wrong, don't trust complex calculations.

üí° Better approach: Have AI write formulas you can verify, not just give you answers.

üñºÔ∏è Image Understanding: The Two-Step Rule

Critical Rule: Transcribe ‚Üí Verify ‚Üí Analyze

Example: Blood Test Results
‚ùå BAD: Upload image ‚Üí "Are my results healthy?"
‚Üí AI might misread values

‚úÖ GOOD:
1. Upload image ‚Üí "Transcribe all values into a table"
2. Verify numbers match the image
3. "Now analyze these results. What's concerning? What's good?"

‚ö†Ô∏è Real risk: AI misreading "100" as "1000" in medical results could lead to wrong conclusions. Always verify transcription first!

üé≠ Hallucinations: How to Catch Them

Why AI Hallucinates
AI is trained to always give an answer‚Äîeven when it doesn't know. It would rather make something up than say "I don't know."
üîç How to Catch Hallucinations
  • Ask for sources/citations
  • Cross-check with Google
  • Use search tools for facts
  • Ask AI: "How confident are you? What could be wrong?"
  • Compare outputs from 2 AIs
‚úÖ Prevention Strategies
  • Add to prompts: "Only use information I've provided"
  • Add: "If unsure, say 'I don't have enough information'"
  • Upload source documents
  • Use newer models (less hallucination)
  • Verify critical info always

üí° Rule of thumb: If it's important, verify it. If it's critical, verify it twice. If it's life/death or legal, don't rely on AI alone.

üîí Privacy & Data Security

‚ö†Ô∏è What You Paste Can Be Used for Training
Free/Plus tiers of ChatGPT and Claude may use your conversations to improve models (unless you opt out).
‚úÖ Safe to Share
  • Public information
  • Your own writing (non-confidential)
  • Public documents and reports
  • General questions
  • Learning and practice materials
‚ùå Don't Share (Unless Approved)
  • Customer data (names, emails, PII)
  • Internal financials
  • Proprietary strategies
  • Confidential documents
  • Passwords or credentials (obviously!)

üí° Enterprise solutions: ChatGPT Enterprise and Claude for Work have privacy guarantees and don't train on your data. Ask your IT team if available.

üß† Thinking Models: When to Use Them

‚úÖ Best For:

  • Logic puzzles
  • When the first answer is wrong
‚ö†Ô∏è Not Needed For:
  • Simple questions
  • Writing tasks
  • Summarizing documents
  • Brainstorming ideas
  • Quick lookups
Available Thinking Models (Oct 2025):

üí° Trade-off: Thinking models take 30 seconds to 2 minutes. Worth it for hard problems, overkill for simple tasks.

üíª Coding Agents: The Reality

Three Levels of AI-Assisted Coding:
1. Autocomplete (Cursor, Copilot) ‚Üí Best for experienced coders
You write, AI suggests next lines

2. Vibe Coding (Claude Code, ChatGPT Code) ‚Üí Good for boilerplate
"Build a contact form" ‚Üí AI generates code

3. Fully Autonomous Agents ‚Üí Still not production-ready
Too many errors, misunderstandings, security issues
‚úÖ Agents Work Well For:
  • Boilerplate code (forms, CRUD apps)
  • Common patterns (well-documented online)
  • Languages you're learning
  • Non-critical prototypes
‚ö†Ô∏è Agents Struggle With:
  • Novel code (never written before)
  • Custom architectures
  • Production-grade security
  • Complex debugging

üí° Bottom line: AI coding tools are amazing assistants. They're not replacing engineers yet. Human oversight is essential.

‚úÖ Verification Checklist: Catch Errors Early

üöß Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

‚ùå Pitfall #1: Trusting Without Verifying
Risk: AI confidently states wrong facts

Fix: Always verify critical info. Add "cite sources" to prompts.
‚ùå Pitfall #2: One Giant Prompt
Risk: AI gets confused, output is generic

Fix: Chain small prompts. Each builds on the last.
‚ùå Pitfall #3: Wrong Tool for the Job
Risk: Using ChatGPT for 100-page doc analysis

Fix: Match tool to task (see decision tree slide)
‚ùå Pitfall #4: Forgetting to Iterate
Risk: Accepting mediocre first output

Fix: "Make it shorter/longer/more specific/different tone"
‚ùå Pitfall #5: Sharing Sensitive Data
Risk: Privacy breach, policy violation

Fix: Check company policy. Use Enterprise tier if available.
‚ùå Pitfall #6: Not Saving What Works
Risk: Re-inventing the wheel every time

Fix: Save successful prompts as templates or Custom GPTs

üõ°Ô∏è Safe Prompting Checklist

Add These to Your Prompts:
  • ‚úì "Double-check all facts and flag uncertainties"
  • ‚úì "Cite sources for any claims"
  • ‚úì "If you don't have enough information, say so"
  • ‚úì "Show your reasoning and any assumptions"
  • ‚úì "Focus only on information in the documents I provided"
Example Safe Prompt:
You are an analyst helping me understand this market report.

Analyze the document and provide:
1. Key findings (3 bullets)
2. Data-driven insights (cite specific numbers)
3. Potential concerns or limitations in the data

Important: Only use information from this document. If you need to make assumptions, state them clearly. Double-check all numbers.
